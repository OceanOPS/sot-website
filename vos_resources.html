VOS Operational Resources — the complete toolkit for ship-based observation management

Your complete toolkit for VOS operations

This page brings together all the operational and technical resources that support the day-to-day work of VOS Program Managers, Port Meteorological Officers, and ships' observers. Resources are organised by theme. Use the navigation links to jump directly to the section you need.

Metadata & VOS Lists

Accurate, up-to-date metadata is fundamental to the correct interpretation of ship observations. The global VOS fleet is recorded in international databases that PMOs and VOS Program Managers are responsible for maintaining.

WIGOS Metadata Requirements:

  • Pub47 Metadata Format Version 4 (PDF) → (current standard)
  • WIGOS Metadata Format Version 1 →

VOS Databases:

  • OceanOPS Metadata Database →

Electronic Logbook Software

Electronic logbook software replaces paper observation registers and automates key tasks: calculating true wind, mean sea level pressure, and dew point; quality-checking entries; coding observations for transmission; and formatting data for digitisation. Two internationally supported packages:

  • TurboWin (KNMI) → (most widely used; open source on GitLab)
  • OBSJMA (Japan Meteorological Agency) →

Transmitting Observations

VOS observations are transmitted by email (preferred) or via Inmarsat-C satellite using Special Access Code 41 (SAC41) through Land Earth Stations (LES) that accept BBXX. The following resource lists all currently participating LES:

  • List of Land Earth Stations that accept BBXX →

Forms & Tools

Standardised forms ensure that recruitment, inspection, and de-recruitment data are captured consistently across the global network.

  • VOSP001 — Foreign VOS Inspection Form (DOC) → (For inspections of ships recruited by another country. Completed forms should be emailed to the national VOS Focal Point of the ship's country of recruitment.)
  • VOSP002 — VOS Metadata Collection Form (ZIP) → (For collecting full metadata during recruitment and inspection visits.)

Siting of Instruments

Correct sensor placement on a ship is critical to data quality — particularly for wind and dew point sensors, where ship superstructure can distort airflow.

  • Making Climate Quality Observations at Sea (NOAA Technical Memorandum) →
  • SAMOS Met Tech Short Course (instrument installation training) →

Certificates & Awards

The Ship Observations Team issues a Certificate of Appreciation for ships that have made a sustained and significant contribution to the VOS Scheme. PMOs may present certificates to qualifying ships in their fleet. Guidelines and issuing criteria are included in the download. Email a copy of each certificate issued to: sot-certificate@groups.wmo.int

  • SOT Certificate of Appreciation (ZIP — pre-signed; request password from TC) →

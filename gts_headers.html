GTS Headers — the technical backbone of real-time data exchange

WMO GTS Headers for SOT Data

The Global Telecommunication System (GTS) is the WMO's operational data exchange backbone, distributing weather and oceanographic observations between national meteorological services and global forecast centres in real time. Data distributed on the GTS is organised using a system of abbreviated headings (TTAAii CCCC) that identify the type, subtype, and originating centre of each bulletin.

For SOT observations — VOS ship weather reports, ASAP upper-air profiles, and SOOP XBT data — specific GTS header conventions apply. National meteorological services must use the correct headers when collecting observations from ships and distributing them on the GTS to ensure that they are correctly received, processed, and archived by receiving centres.

VOS / Ship Weather Report Headers (BBXX)

Surface ship weather reports encoded in the traditional FM13-SHIP format use the BBXX bulletin identifier. Ships' observations collected from the GTS or received by national LES are assembled into ship bulletins and distributed under appropriate SHIP bulletin headers before onward routing on the GTS.

ASAP Upper-Air Headers (TEMP SHIP)

Upper-air profiles from ASAP ships are encoded in TEMP SHIP format and distributed on the GTS using TEMP SHIP bulletin headers (UUSS or UUXX series, depending on the level of completeness). Inmarsat C is the primary transmission method; data reach national collection centres and are forwarded to the GTS within approximately one hour of the sounding.

SOOP / XBT Headers (BATHY)

XBT temperature profiles collected by SOOP ships are encoded in BATHY format and distributed on the GTS under BATHY bulletin headers. Real-time profiles are typically available on the GTS within 24 hours of collection following automated quality control.

GDACs — archiving the ship observation record for science and the future

Global Data Collection Centres — the long-term archive of ship data

Real-time ship observations distributed on the GTS serve immediate operational needs — they are assimilated into forecast models within hours of collection and then, from the GTS, are routinely archived. But the long-term scientific value of ship observations — for climate research, reanalysis, and trend detection — depends on a parallel system of higher-quality delayed-mode data processing and archival.

For VOS data, this delayed-mode archive is managed through the Marine Climate Data System (MCDS) and its two Global Data Collection Centres (GDACs) located at the German Weather Service (DWD) in Offenbach, Germany, and the UK Met Office in Exeter, United Kingdom.

National VOS Program Managers are responsible for submitting their national fleet's delayed-mode data to both GDACs every quarter, in International Maritime Meteorological Tape (IMMT) format, following Minimum Quality Control (MQC) procedures. The GDACs then ensure uniform quality standards and distribute the data to eight regional centres responsible for producing Marine Climatological Summaries for specific ocean areas.

What delayed-mode data adds

Delayed-mode processing allows for quality control procedures that are not possible in real time: comparison with climatological baselines, visual inspection of suspect observations, and correction of instrument calibration offsets identified after the fact. The result is a higher-quality dataset that forms the backbone of long-term climate records.

Ship observations in the Marine Climatological Summaries Scheme (MCSS) extend back to the mid-nineteenth century, making them among the longest continuous in-situ climate records in existence. The International Comprehensive Ocean-Atmosphere Data Set (ICOADS) — the world's primary historical marine climate archive — is built substantially on delayed-mode ship observations submitted through the MCSS.

Submitting data to the GDACs

Submission requirements for national VOS Program Managers:

  • Data must be quality controlled in accordance with WMO Minimum Quality Control (MQC) guidelines before submission. The current standard is MQCS-7. MQC software (MQC-5) is available from WMO.
  • Data must be submitted electronically each quarter in IMMT format to both Global Data Collection Centres. Programme Managers should advise the GDACs when data are available.
    • GDAC Germany (DWD) — gcc@dwd.de
    • GDAC United Kingdom (UK Met Office) — gcc@metoffice.gov.uk

SOOP and ASAP data archives

Upper ocean thermal data from SOOP/XBT deployments are archived through the Global Temperature-Salinity Profile Programme (GTSPP) and through the Coriolis GDAC (France) and the US National Centers for Environmental Information (NCEI). ASAP upper-air data are archived through the ECMWF and national meteorological service archives.
